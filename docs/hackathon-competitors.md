## Notable Competitors (Most Feature Overlap with Periscope)

### Sigil
Closest direct competitor. Also a solo-dev tribe coordination tool with on-chain diplomacy, intel, fuel monitoring, 3D galaxy map, and reputation. Built in Elixir (~27K lines) with 5 Move contracts. Key differences: Sigil uses Seal encryption for its intel marketplace (vs our NaCl sealed boxes), and has a reputation engine we don't have. We have custom currencies/markets, gate tolls, treasuries, mining/DPS tracking, and game log integration that Sigil doesn't appear to have.

### EF webCore
Broad platform with 11 "cores" covering ship fitting, fleet intel, industry, base construction, tribe discovery, and dev infrastructure. Pulls from both Stillness and Utopia. More of a data aggregation platform than an organizational toolkit. No on-chain features like our markets, standings registries, or treasuries.

### Eve Frontier Ops
No-backend React+Vite app with jobs board, bounty board, assembly management, starmap (24,500+ systems), AI mission control. Similar "no backend" philosophy. Less focused on on-chain smart contracts.

### Frontier BaseOps
Operations control plane for bases. Integrates gates, storage, delegated execution, live events. More focused on operational workflows than our broader toolkit approach.

### Covenant
Interesting diplomatic approach with collateral-backed treaties and graduated penalties via killmail monitoring. Complementary to our standings system rather than competitive.

### FEN (Frontier Exchange Network)
Most sophisticated competing market system. Player-owned corridors, toll collection, AMM pools. 6 contracts, 117 tests. Different approach from our order-book markets.

### StarLane / Silk Road Protocol
Gate toll competitors, but narrower scope (just gates/tolls vs our full organizational toolkit).

### HUGINN
AI assistant embedded in SSUs. Not directly competitive but interesting use of Claude Sonnet for in-game intelligence.
