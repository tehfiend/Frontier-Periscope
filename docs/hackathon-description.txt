Frontier Periscope is an organizational toolkit and real-time monitoring platform for EVE Frontier. Eight Move smart contracts deployed on testnet, zero backend, everything runs in your browser.
-----
Periscope lets players create their own custom currencies and use them as a real medium of exchange. You pick a name and symbol and Periscope patches the Move bytecode and publishes the contract on chain entirely in the browser. No CLI, no gas station, no dev environment needed. This is the only project in the hackathon doing in-browser Move compilation and the only one where custom tokens actually circulate as money through markets, tolls, and treasuries. These currencies power a full on-chain economy. Players create order book markets at any Smart Storage Unit where items and custom currency trade through atomic escrow, zero trust required. Players can acquire your currency by trading SUI on the exchange or by selling ore and resources at your market. Gates charge tolls in your currency. Treasury wallets collect revenue in multiple currencies with multi-admin controls. You can even back your currency with provable escrow reserves to create a true reserve currency.
-----
The on-chain standings and diplomacy system is the access control layer that ties everything together. On-chain registries on a seven-point scale determine who can trade at your markets, pass through your gates, and access your storage units. Every Smart Assembly extension Periscope deploys reads from these registries so one diplomatic change cascades across all your infrastructure.
-----
The encrypted private map system lets players share structure locations on chain without exposing them publicly. Location data is encrypted with X25519 sealed boxes and stored on chain as ciphertext. Encryption keys are derived deterministically from wallet signatures so there's no key management and no local key storage at all. Members receive invite objects containing the map key encrypted with their wallet's public key. Only invited members can decrypt the locations. The data lives on chain but is completely unreadable without the right key.
-----
Eight Move contracts deployed on testnet, all working together:
- Market: on-chain order books with atomic escrow for items and custom currencies
- Treasury: multi-admin, multi-currency shared wallets for organizational finance
- Standings: seven-point diplomatic registries with multi-admin support
- Gate Toll: configurable tolls in any custom currency with standing thresholds for free passage
- Turret Extensions: custom targeting weights deployed via in-browser bytecode patching
- SSU Integration: standings-gated access control for storage units
- Private Maps: on-chain encrypted location storage with invite-based and standings-gated access modes
- Token Factory: in-browser Move bytecode patching for custom currency deployment
-----
A player creates a currency, deposits it in a treasury, sets up a market, deploys toll and standings extensions on their gate, and shares their location on an encrypted map. One toolkit, eight contracts.
-----
Fully client-side. No backend server, no database, no accounts, no API keys. Data stays in IndexedDB and every chain interaction goes directly through public Sui endpoints. Every transaction is signed locally and submitted directly to the Sui network.
-----
Sonar provides real-time monitoring from two channels: blockchain polling every 15 seconds via Sui GraphQL and local game log tailing via File System Access API. Tracks combat, trades, fuel levels, gate activity, structure changes, mining efficiency, and DPS. Configurable watchlists with distinct alert sounds per event type. The log analyzer parses mining cycles with ore-per-minute rates, combat encounters with per-target DPS breakdowns, cargo-full detection, and weapon analysis, all live during gameplay.
-----
Also includes a 3D Star Map with WebGL jump route planning, structure management with fuel tracking and Move extension deployment from the browser, and it installs as a standalone desktop app via Chrome or Edge PWA.
